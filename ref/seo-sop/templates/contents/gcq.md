
# 🦁 Growth Spec: Google Content Quality Protocol (GCQ)

**Objective:** To strictly adhere to Google's "Helpful, Reliable, People-First" content standards.
**Impact:** This protocol is the primary defense against Algorithm Updates (Core Updates) and the key to AdSense approval.

---

## 1. The Core Philosophy: "People-First"
**The Golden Rule:** Content must be created primarily to help people, not to manipulate search rankings.

| ✅ People-First (Do This) | ❌ Search Engine-First (Avoid This) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Writing for an existing, intended audience. | Creating content on "Trending Topics" outside your niche just for clicks. |
| Demonstrating first-hand expertise and depth. | Summarizing what others say without adding value. |
| The site has a clear primary purpose. | Mass-producing content on disparate topics to game volume. |
| Reader leaves feeling "Satisfied". | Reader leaves feeling they need to search again. |

---

## 2. The "Who, How, Why" Transparency Framework
Google explicitly asks these three questions to assess credibility. Every article template MUST address them.

### A. WHO (Created this?)
*   **Requirement:** It must be self-evident to visitors who authored the content.
*   **Action Items:**
    *   **Bylines:** Every post must have an author name (`By Edwin`).
    *   **Author Page:** A dedicated page (`/about` or `/author/edwin`) detailing background and credentials.
    *   **Bio:** A short bio block at the end of the article.

### B. HOW (Was it created?)
*   **Requirement:** Readers want to know the effort and methodology behind the content.
*   **Action Items:**
    *   **Disclosure:** If AI was used for drafting, disclose it. (e.g., "Assisted by AI, Verified by Experts").
    *   **Evidence:** For reviews/tutorials, show *proof of work*. (Original photos, screenshots of the process, video clips).
    *   **Methodology:** Briefly explain how data was collected.

### C. WHY (Was it created?)
*   **Requirement:** The primary purpose must be to help the user.
*   **Action Items:**
    *   Avoid "Traffic Traps" (writing about a release date when one doesn't exist).
    *   Ensure the H1 promises exactly what the content delivers.

## 3. E-E-A-T Compliance (The Trust Metric)
**E-E-A-T** is not a ranking factor, but a lens through which Quality Raters evaluate results.

1.  **Experience (第一手经验)**: Does the content prove you actually used the product or visited the place?
    *   *Fix:* Use phrases like "In my testing...", "I encountered...". Use original media.
2.  **Expertise (专业知识)**: Do you have the skill to talk about this?
    *   *Fix:* Link to your GitHub/Portfolio. Use industry-standard terminology correctly.
3.  **Authoritativeness (权威性)**: Is your site a go-to source for this topic?
    *   *Fix:* Stick to your niche. Don't write about "Cooking" on a "Coding" blog.
4.  **Trustworthiness (可信度)**: Is the info accurate and safe?
    *   *Fix:* Cite sources. Use HTTPS. No factual errors.

## 4. The 32-Point Quality Audit (Self-Assessment)
Before publishing, run the content through this gauntlet.

**Content & Value**
- [ ] Does it provide **original** reporting, research, or analysis?
- [ ] Does it provide a **substantial/comprehensive** description of the topic?
- [ ] Does it offer **insightful analysis** beyond the obvious?
- [ ] Is it **better** than the other pages in search results?
- [ ] Does the headline avoid being **exaggerating** or shocking (Clickbait)?
- [ ] Is this the sort of page you'd want to **bookmark** or share?

**Production Quality**
- [ ] Is it free of **spelling** or stylistic issues?
- [ ] Was it produced well, or does it look **sloppy/hasty**?
- [ ] Is it **mass-produced** by a large number of creators (content farm)?
- [ ] Does it have an excessive amount of **ads** that distract from the main content?

**Reliability**
- [ ] Are there clear **sources** for data?
- [ ] Is there evidence of the **expertise** involved?
- [ ] Are there easily-verified **factual errors**?

---

## 5. AdSense "Low Quality" Avoidance (The Value Add)
AdSense often rejects sites for "Low Value Content". To avoid this:

*   **Avoid "Scraping"**: Never just copy-paste specifications from Amazon or a manufacturer. You must add *opinion* or *synthesis*.
*   **Avoid "Thin Content"**: Pages with 2 sentences and 10 ads.
*   **The "Value Add" Rule**: If I can find this exact text on Wikipedia or ChatGPT, why does your page exist? You must add:
    *   Curated organization.
    *   Personal perspective.
    *   Actionable tools/calculators.
    *   Visual aids (Charts/Diagrams).
